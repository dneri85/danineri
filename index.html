My story

I didn't just find my path — I built it, city by city.

A journey shaped by curiosity, a lot of uprooting, and the stubborn belief that the next chapter is always better than the last.

🇧🇷

The beginning

Born in Brazil, wired for curiosity

Before I ever thought about systems, workflows, or efficiency — I was just a kid obsessed with how things fit together. Legos were basically my first job. Structure, logic, the quiet satisfaction of making pieces click into place. I didn't know it then, but that instinct would follow me everywhere.

🗽

Age 14 — New York

Everything changed overnight

At 14, my family packed up and chased the American dream to New York. New language. New culture. New everything. It was disorienting, humbling, and — looking back — one of the best things that ever happened to me. You learn real adaptability when you have no choice but to figure it out. No manual, no fallback, just forward.

🎸

2012 — Tennessee

Memphis, logistics, and thinking at scale

I got married, settled in Tennessee, and landed in the middle of one of the world's great logistics hubs. Memphis moves things — millions of packages, truckloads, supply chains, all flowing through this one city. I was hooked. Watching complex operations run (or fail to run) at that scale lit something up in me: a genuine obsession with efficiency, coordination, and making systems actually work.

🌉

2016 — California

Operations, evolved

West Coast, new chapter. I kept building on what I'd learned — but the work got sharper. Less about moving boxes, more about improving how people and systems interact. Workflows, processes, the invisible friction that slows organizations down. If something could run smoother, I wanted to know why it wasn't — and how to fix it.

✦

Today

Where it's all coming together

Operations. Data. Healthcare. Three things that, when they actually work in harmony, don't just improve a process — they improve someone's life. That's where my focus is now: building systems that are more efficient, experiences that feel seamless, and outcomes that genuinely matter. The kid who loved Legos is still in there, just playing with much bigger pieces.
